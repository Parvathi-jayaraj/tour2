Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is a captivating coastal city in the southern Indian state of Kerala. Renowned for its rich cultural heritage, historical significance, and natural beauty, Kozhikode has played a pivotal role in shaping the history of the Malabar region. It was famously known as the "City of Spices" during ancient times due to its prominence as a major trading hub for spices, attracting merchants from Arabia, China, and Europe.
