Popular Streets

'SM Street'

S.M. Street (short for Sweet Meat Street), located in the heart of Kozhikode (Calicut), Kerala, is one of the oldest and most vibrant commercial streets in the region. It is a bustling hub of trade, culture, and history, drawing locals and tourists alike. The street derives its name from the sweetmeat (halwa) shops set up by Gujarati traders during the colonial era. The iconic Kozhikode Halwa, a sticky, colorful dessert, remains a hallmark of the street to this day. S.M. Street is deeply rooted in Kozhikode's rich trading history, having served as a prominent marketplace during the time of the Zamorins (local rulers) and later during Portuguese, Dutch, and British rule. S.M. Street is a paradise for shoppers, offering a mix of traditional and modern goods. Items such as textiles, jewelry, spices, handicrafts, and electronics are readily available.
Opening Hours: 24 hrs

'Gujrati Street'

Gujarati Street, located near the Kozhikode Beach in Kerala, is a historical and culturally significant area that reflects the longstanding connection between the Gujarati trading community and the city of Kozhikode. Over time, the street became a vibrant cultural enclave, showcasing the unique fusion of Gujarati and Kerala traditions. The area is home to Jain temples, reflecting the religious practices of the Gujarati community. Gujarati Street is known for its unique food offerings, including snacks like dhoklas, the famous Kozhikode Halwa (popularized by Gujarati traders), and other vegetarian delicacies. While retaining its old-world charm, Gujarati Street has adapted to modern times, with newer shops and businesses catering to contemporary needs. It remains a must-visit spot for anyone exploring the rich history and diverse culture of Kozhikode.
